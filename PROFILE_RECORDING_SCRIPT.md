# Voice Profile Recording Script

Use this script for speaker enrollment. Read it naturally at normal volume, about 20-30 cm from the microphone. Do not whisper. Leave a quiet room tone for one second before and after speaking.

Recommended recording length: 25-40 seconds.

## Primary Script

Hello, this is my voice profile for the lab assistant. Today I am speaking clearly and naturally so the system can recognize my voice during experiments.

In the lab, I may ask about buffers, samples, pipettes, centrifuges, incubators, reagents, microscope images, temperature, pressure, and timing.

Please prepare five milliliters of sodium chloride solution, label tube A seventeen, check the pH, and start the timer for thirty seconds.

I might say short commands, longer explanations, numbers like one, two, three, nine, fifteen, forty-two, one hundred, and phrases with different sounds like fresh frozen fraction, bright blue buffer, quick calibration, and gentle vortex mixing.

This is still my normal speaking voice. The assistant should focus on me when I am talking and ignore unrelated background speech.

## Short Retake Script

This is my normal lab voice. Please recognize me when I ask for measurements, protocols, sample labels, reagent names, numbers, and short commands. I will speak naturally, clearly, and at a steady pace.

## Recording Tips

- Record 25-40 seconds for a first profile.
- Use your normal voice, not an exaggerated announcer voice.
- Keep the microphone position similar to real use.
- Avoid music, fan noise, keyboard noise, and other people talking.
- If possible, record two profiles later: one quiet-room profile and one realistic lab profile.
